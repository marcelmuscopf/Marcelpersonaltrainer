Why Do a Biomechanical Assessment?

Every body is unique. Understanding how yours moves is the first step toward building a truly effective training plan. A biomechanical assessment goes beyond generic workouts – it analyzes your posture, joint angles, movement patterns, and muscle imbalances to identify exactly what your body needs to move better, feel better, and perform better.

Two people can do the same exercise and experience totally different results – not because of effort, but because of structure. For example:

  • Squats: A person whose legs are proportionally longer than their arm span may need a wider stance and heel elevation to maintain balance and depth, as their center of mass shifts differently during the movement.
  • Running: Someone with wider hips may require greater glute activation to stabilize the pelvis and protect the knees from excessive inward movement — a key factor in preventing joint overload during running or repetitive impact activities.

These differences matter. That’s why I tailor every program based on real movement data — no guesswork. The goal is to make sure you move well, avoid injury, and get stronger with purpose. A biomechanical test also helps determine which types of exercises and modifications are best suited for your current physical condition, especially if you're experiencing a reduction in mobility, strength, or stability. This way, training becomes both effective and safe for your unique needs.
